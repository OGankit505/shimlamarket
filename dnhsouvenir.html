Warli Painting

Warli painting is a tribal art form that originated in the Warli tribes of Maharashtra, but it is also prevalent in Dadra and Nagar Haveli. This art form typically involves creating intricate, monochromatic patterns and scenes depicting rural life, rituals, and nature on mud or cloth surfaces.

Terracotta Craft

Terracotta pottery and sculpture making is a traditional craft practiced by local artisans. They create a variety of utilitarian items, as well as decorative figurines, using clay and traditional firing techniques.

Coconut Shell Craft

Due to the abundance of coconuts in the coastal regions, artisans create a variety of crafts from coconut shells. These can include utensils, jewelry, and decorative items.

Pithora Painting

Pithora paintings are a form of ritualistic art practiced by the Rathwa and Bhil tribes. These vibrant paintings often depict deities, rituals, and scenes from daily life, and they are traditionally done on walls or fabric.
